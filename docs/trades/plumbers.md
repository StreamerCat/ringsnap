## Recommended Custom Instructions

Copy and customize this for your business:

```
Always ask if there's active water damage or flooding - those calls are emergencies.
Mention we offer 24/7 emergency service with no extra charge for after-hours calls.
We're licensed, bonded, and insured with [X] years of experience.
For water heater calls, ask how old the current unit is - if 10+ years, suggest replacement options.
Our service area is [your area] - politely redirect out-of-area callers.
We offer free estimates for new installations.
```

### Emergency Detection

Add specific triggers for your AI to recognize emergencies:

```
Treat these as emergencies requiring immediate transfer:
- Burst or broken pipe
- Major water leak
- Flooding or water damage
- Sewage backup
- No water in the house
- Gas smell near water heater
```

## Setting Up Emergency Handling

### Emergency Policy Example

```
For active flooding, burst pipes, or sewage backup:
1. Transfer immediately to my cell: (303) 555-1234
2. If no answer after 3 rings, text me with caller details
3. Tell the customer I'll call back within 5 minutes

For urgent but non-emergency (no hot water, slow drain):
- Schedule for same day or next morning
- Text me the booking details
```

## Pricing Questions

Plumbing pricing varies widely. Here's how to handle:

### What to Tell Your AI

```
For pricing questions, explain that we provide free estimates 
because every job is different. We can give a rough range:
- Drain cleaning: $99-199 depending on complexity
- Water heater repair: Starting at $150 for diagnostics
- Water heater replacement: $800-2,500 installed
- Faucet installation: $150-300

But emphasize a technician needs to assess the actual situation.
```

## Seasonal Considerations

### Winter (Frozen Pipes Season)

Update custom instructions:
```
For frozen pipe calls, ask if water is still flowing or completely stopped.
Mention we can thaw pipes and help prevent re-freezing.
Frozen but not burst = same day priority. Burst = emergency transfer.
```

### Summer (Water Heater Season)

```
Water heater issues are common in summer. 
Ask about unit age - if 10+ years, mention we can discuss upgrade options.
No hot water in summer is inconvenient but not an emergency.
```
