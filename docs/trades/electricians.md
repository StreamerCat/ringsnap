### Safety-Critical Detection

```
EMERGENCY - Transfer immediately for:
- Sparks or arcing
- Burning smell from outlets or panel
- Smoke from electrical
- Someone was shocked or electrocuted
- Buzzing/humming from panel
- Hot outlets or switches
- Water near electrical panel

Tell caller to stay away from the affected area and don't touch it.
```

## Emergency Handling

### Emergency Policy Example

```
For electrical emergencies (sparks, burning, smoke, shock):
1. Tell caller NOT to touch anything electrical
2. If active fire or smoke, tell them to call 911 first
3. Transfer immediately to my cell: (303) 555-1234

For power outages:
- Check if neighbors have power (utility issue vs house issue)
- If house only, book same-day priority
- If widespread, may be utility - suggest calling power company
```

## High-Value Services

### EV Charger Installations

EV chargers are a growing, profitable segment. Train your AI:

```
For EV charger inquiries:
- Ask what type of vehicle (helps determine charger needs)
- Ask if they have a 240V outlet in garage already
- Ask approximate distance from electrical panel to garage
- Mention we're certified for Tesla, ChargePoint, and all major brands
- Schedule a free site evaluation
- Typical installations: $500-2,000 depending on complexity
```

### Panel Upgrades

```
For panel upgrade inquiries:
- Ask current panel amperage if known (usually 100A or 200A)
- Ask why they're considering (adding circuits, selling home, EV charger)
- Older homes (pre-1980) often need upgrades for safety
- Schedule a free evaluation
- Typical upgrades: $1,500-3,500
```

### Generator Installations

```
For generator inquiries:
- Ask if they want whole-home or partial coverage
- Ask about primary concerns (power outages, medical equipment, work from home)
- Mention we handle permits and inspections
- Schedule consultation for sizing
- Typical installations: $3,000-15,000+ depending on size
```

## Older Home Considerations

Many electrical calls come from older homes. Train your AI:

```
For homes built before 1980:
- May have outdated wiring (aluminum, knob-and-tube)
- Panels may need upgrade for modern loads
- GFCI outlets may be needed in kitchens/baths
- Suggest a whole-home electrical inspection

For homes built before 1960:
- Strong recommendation for electrical safety inspection
- Often have ungrounded outlets
- May have fire hazards
```

## Permit and Licensing Questions

Electrical work often requires permits. Be prepared:

```
For permit questions:
- Yes, we pull all required permits
- Permits protect homeowners and ensure work passes inspection
- Unpermitted electrical work can affect home insurance and resale
- Our work is done to code and inspected
- Permit costs are included in our estimates
```
